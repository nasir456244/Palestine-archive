Palestine Before the Nakba

Rare Photographs from 1900–1948

Introduction

Welcome to this curated collection of historical Palestinian photographs from before the Nakba of 1948. Each image has been selected to provide a window into the landscapes, daily life, cultural practices, and political moments that shaped Palestine’s history. Our goal is to preserve and share these precious glimpses of the past, giving context and meaning to each photograph. Explore, learn, and appreciate the resilience and richness of Palestinian heritage.

Landscapes & Cities

Explore the diverse cities and natural vistas of Palestine before 1948, capturing the essence of urban life and scenic surroundings.

The bustling marketplace in Jaffa, circa 1900, viewed from Clock Tower Square. In the background, Mahmoudiya Mosque stands as a historical landmark, highlighting the city’s vibrant commercial and cultural life.(Library of Congress)
Panoramic view of Haifa and its port in 1940, capturing the city’s landscape, harbor activity, and urban development during the British Mandate period.
A street vendor in Jerusalem during the 1920s selling freshly baked pita bread, showcasing everyday commerce and the lively atmosphere of the Old City. (Frank Scholten)
A view of Gaza in the 1930s, reflecting the city’s coastal architecture, local life, and urban landscape before major mid-century changes. Source: palestineremembered.com
Saffuriya, perched on a hill near Nazareth and home to over 4,000 Palestinians, with historical significance dating back to Roman and Crusader periods. The village exemplifies the region’s strategic and cultural importance.(Library of Congress)
Daily Life

A glimpse into the routines, traditions, and social customs of Palestinian communities during the early 20th century.

In 1922, Palestinian farmers in the West Bank carefully picked olives, continuing a centuries-old tradition. The harvest was both an economic activity and a cultural celebration, bringing together families and communities.
A family group from Bethlehem, photographed by Khalil Raad around 1930, showing familial bonds, traditional clothing, and daily life in early 20th-century Palestine.
A newlywed couple in Ain Karem, 1936, captured on their wedding day, showcasing traditional Palestinian attire and customs during matrimonial celebrations.
Schoolchildren in Hebron, 1930, pictured in their classroom, representing the educational environment and social structures of Palestinian towns during this period.
Women grinding grain at a local mill in Palestine, circa 1900, reflecting traditional food preparation techniques and daily labor practices in rural communities.
Cultural & Religious

Discover the rich cultural practices, attire, religious landmarks, and ceremonies that shaped Palestinian identity.

A panoramic view of Jerusalem’s Old City in the 1920s, highlighting the Dome of the Rock and Masjid Al-Aqsa, central symbols of cultural and religious heritage.
Photographed by Yousef Albina, this image captures the Dome of the Rock between the 1920s and 1930s, showcasing its enduring significance in Jerusalem’s religious and urban landscape.
A young woman from Ramallah in 1920, wearing traditional Arabic attire that reflects the region’s cultural identity and fashion of the era.
Bedouin farmers performing the traditional Dabke dance during a wedding in the Negev, 1933, celebrating community, heritage, and festive customs.
A Palestinian woman weaving a basket between 1910 and 1914, captured by National Geographic, illustrating daily craftsmanship, resourcefulness, and cultural traditions.
Political & Historical Moments

Witness pivotal events, resistance movements, and historical milestones that influenced Palestine prior to 1948.

Troops stationed near Haifa in 1936 performing railway protection duties during a period of political unrest, reflecting the tensions and strategic importance of infrastructure in Palestine.
Palestinian residents voting to support a general strike proposed by the Arab Higher Committee in 1936, calling for an end to Jewish immigration and land dispossession, and demonstrating political mobilization against colonial policies.
Insurgents participating in the 1936 revolt against British Mandate authorities and growing Zionist settlement, highlighting the resistance and struggle for Palestinian autonomy.
Palestinian leaders meeting with Amin al-Husseini, the Grand Mufti of Jerusalem, discussing political strategies and responses to British rule and regional challenges prior to 1948.
Over 750,000 Palestinians were forcibly expelled from their homes in 1948 during the Nakba, representing a pivotal moment of displacement and upheaval in Palestinian history.(AFP)
